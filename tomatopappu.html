Tomatopappu

Tomato pappu is a popular South Indian dish made with toor dal (split pigeon peas) and tomatoes. It is a flavorful and nutritious dish that is easy to prepare and goes well with steamed rice, roti, or naan. Toor dal is a good source of protein and dietary fiber, which makes this dish a healthy choice for vegetarians and vegans. Tomatoes are also a good source of vitamin C, vitamin K, and potassium, which help to boost the immune system, support bone health, and regulate blood pressure.

And this is how my family makes Tomatopappu...

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)

2 medium-sized tomatoes, chopped

1 onion, chopped

2-3 green chillies, slit

1 tsp ginger paste

1 tsp garlic paste

1/2 tsp turmeric powder

1 tsp red chilli powder

1 tsp cumin powder

1 tsp coriander powder

Salt, to taste

3 cups water

2 tbsp oil or ghee

A handful of curry leaves

1 tsp mustard seeds

1 tsp cumin seeds

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Rinse the toor dal thoroughly and add it to a pressure cooker. Add 2 cups of water and pressure cook for 3-4 whistles or until the dal is soft and mushy.

STEP 2:

In a pan, heat oil or ghee and add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them crackle.

STEP 3:

Add chopped onions, green chillies, curry leaves, and ginger-garlic paste. Saut√© until the onions turn translucent.

STEP 4:

Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chilli powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook until the tomatoes turn soft and mushy.

STEP 5:

Add the cooked toor dal to the tomato mixture and mix well.

STEP 6:

Add 1 cup of water or as needed to achieve the desired consistency. Let it simmer for 5-10 minutes or until the flavors are well combined.

STEP 7:

Serve hot with steamed rice or roti.
