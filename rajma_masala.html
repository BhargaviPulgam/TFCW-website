RAJMA MASALA

Rajma masala is a popular North Indian dish made with red kidney beans (rajma) cooked in a thick, spicy tomato-based gravy. It is typically served with rice or naan bread. The dish is a great source of protein and fiber, making it a popular choice for vegetarians and vegans. Rajma masala is also rich in iron, magnesium, and potassium. The kidney beans used in the dish are known to lower cholesterol levels and aid in digestion.

The following is how my family makes Rajma masala...

BASIC INGREDIENTS:

Rajma (kidney beans)

Onion

Tomato

Garlic

Ginger

Green chili

Cumin seeds

Coriander powder

Turmeric powder

Red chili powder

Garam masala

Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)

Salt

Oil

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

Rajma (Red kidney beans) - 1 cup

Onion - 1 large, finely chopped

Tomatoes - 2 medium, finely chopped

Garlic - 4-5 cloves, minced

Ginger - 1 inch piece, grated

Green chilies - 2-3, finely chopped

Cumin seeds - 1 tsp

Coriander powder - 2 tsp

Red chili powder - 1 tsp

Turmeric powder - 1/2 tsp

Garam masala powder - 1 tsp

Salt - as per taste

Oil - 2-3 tbsp

Water - 2-3 cups

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Soak the rajma in enough water for 6-8 hours or overnight. Drain the water and rinse the rajma thoroughly.

STEP 2:

In a pressure cooker, add the rajma along with 4 cups of water and a pinch of salt. Pressure cook for 4-5 whistles or until the rajma is soft and cooked well.

STEP 3:

In a pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.

STEP 4:

Add finely chopped onions, minced garlic, grated ginger, and finely chopped green chilies. Saute until the onions turn translucent and soft.

STEP 5:

Add finely chopped tomatoes and saute until they turn soft and mushy.

STEP 6:

Add coriander powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook until the spices are well roasted and aromatic.

STEP 7:

Add the cooked rajma along with its water and mix well.

STEP 8:

Add garam masala powder and mix well.

STEP 9:

Let the rajma masala simmer for 10-15 minutes or until the gravy thickens to the desired consistency.

STEP 10:

Serve hot with steamed rice, roti, or naan.
