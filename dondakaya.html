Dondakaya

Dondakaya, also known as Ivy Gourd or Tindora, is a small, elongated, and cylindrical tropical vegetable commonly found in India and other Asian countries. In Indian cuisine, dondakaya is often used in vegetarian dishes due to its high nutritional value and low calorie count. dondakaya is a versatile and nutritious vegetable that can be enjoyed in a variety of ways, and is a popular ingredient in many Indian households.

And this is how my family makes Dondakaya...

BASIC INGREDIENTS:

Dondakaya(Ivy Gourd)

Oil

Mustard seeds

Cumin seeds

Urad dal

Chana dal

Asafoetida(hing)

Dried red chillies

Curry leaves

Turmeric powder

Red chili powder

Salt

Ginger-garlic paste

Onions

Fresh coriander leaves, chopped(for garnish)

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

500g dondakaya(Ivy gourd) , washed and chopped

3 tbsp oil

1 tsp mustard seeds

1 tsp cumin seeds

1 tsp urad dal

1 tsp chana dal

A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

2-3 dried red chillies

10-12 curry leaves

1 tsp turmeric powder

1 tsp red chilli powder (optional)

Salt, to taste

1 tbsp ginger garlic paste

1 onion, chopped

Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.

STEP 2:

Add cumin seeds, urad dal, and chana dal. Fry until the dals turn golden brown.

STEP 3:

Add asafoetida and dried red chillies. Fry for a few seconds.

STEP 4:

Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.

STEP 5:

Add ginger garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.

STEP 6:

Add the chopped dondakaya and mix well. Add turmeric powder, red chilli powder (if using), and salt to taste. Mix everything well.

STEP 7:

Cover and cook for 10-12 minutes or until the dondakaya is cooked and tender, stirring occasionally.

STEP 8:

Turn off the heat and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

STEP 9:

Serve hot as a side dish with rice or roti
