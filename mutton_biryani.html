MUTTON BIRYANI

Hyderabadi mutton biryani is a popular South Indian dish that originated in the Telugu-speaking regions of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. This aromatic and flavorful dish is made with succulent pieces of mutton or lamb, long-grain basmati rice, and a blend of spices and herbs Telugu mutton biryani is typically served with raita, a yogurt-based side dish, and a tangy lemon pickle. It is a beloved dish among food lovers in India and beyond, known for its bold flavors and satisfying texture. Some of the popular regional variations of mutton biryani in India include the Hyderabadi biryani, Kolkata biryani, Lucknowi biryani, Ambur biryani, Malabar biryani, and the Dindigul biryani. The regional variations of mutton biryani reflect the diverse culinary traditions of different parts of India.

The following is how my family makes Mutton Biryani...

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Mutton Biryani

500g mutton, cut into pieces

2 cups basmati rice

3 medium-sized onions, thinly sliced

2 medium-sized tomatoes, chopped

1/2 cup yogurt

2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste

4 green chilies, slit length wise

1/2 cup mint leaves, chopped

1/2 cup coriander leaves, chopped

2 tsp garam masala powder

2 tsp red chili powder

1/2 tsp turmeric powder

1 tsp cumin seeds

3-4 cardamom pods

1-inch cinnamon stick

2 bay leaves

4-5 cloves

A pinch of saffron

1/4 cup oil

1/4 cup ghee

Salt, to taste

4 cups water

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Wash the rice in water and soak it in water for at least 30 minutes.

STEP 2:

In a deep bottomed pan or a pressure cooker, heat oil and ghee together. Add cumin seeds, cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, and cloves. Stir and cook for a few seconds until fragrant.

STEP 3:

Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.

STEP 4:

Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for 1-2 minutes.

STEP 5:

Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.

STEP 6:

Add mutton pieces and mix well. Cook for atleast 15-20 minutes minutes until the mutton is browned.

STEP 7:

Add garam masala powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.

STEP 8:

Add the yogurt and cook for 3-4 minutes until the mutton is well coated with the spices.

STEP 9:

Add chopped mint leaves and coriander leaves. Mix well.

STEP 10:

Add 4 cups of water and bring it to a boil.

STEP 11:

Drain the soaked rice and add it to the mutton mixture. Mix well and cook until the water comes to a boil.

STEP 12:

Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes or until the rice is fully cooked.

STEP 13:

In a small bowl, soak a pinch of saffron in 1-2 tablespoons of milk. Once the biryani is cooked, sprinkle the saffron milk over the biryani.

STEP 14:

Garnish with fried onions and chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot with raita.
