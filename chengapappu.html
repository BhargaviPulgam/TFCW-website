Chengapappu (Chana dal)

Senagapappu, also known as Bengal gram or chana dal, is a type of lentil that is commonly used in Indian and South Asian cuisine. It is a small, yellow-colored legume that is packed with protein, fiber, vitamins, and minerals. It is also a popular ingredient in Middle Eastern and Mediterranean cuisines, where it is used to make hummus and falafel. Senagapappu is a nutritious and flavorful ingredient that can add a healthy boost to any meal.

And this is how my family makes Chengapappu...

BASIC INGREDIENTS:

Chana dal(Bengal gram)

Water

Oil

Mustard seeds

Cumin seeds

Curry leaves

Ginger-garlic paste

Green chillies, slit

Asafoetida (hing)

Turmeric powder

Red chili powder

Dhania powder (coriander powder)

Garam masala

Salt

Fresh coriander leaves, chopped(for garnish)

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

1 chana dal (Bengal gram)

4 cups water

2 tbsp oil

1 tsp mustard seeds

1 tsp cumin seeds

10-12 curry leaves

1 tbsp Ginger-garlic paste

A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

2 green chillies

1/2 tsp turmeric powder

1 1/2 tsp red chilli powder (optional)

1 teaspoon coriander powder

1/2 teaspoon garam masala

Salt, to taste

Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Rinse the chana dal in water and soak it in enough water for at least 30 minutes.

STEP 2:

Drain the water and transfer the chana dal to a pot. Add 4 cups of water and bring it to a boil.

STEP 3:

Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the dal is soft and cooked through. Keep an eye on the water level and add more as needed.

STEP 4:

In a separate pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Let them crackle.

STEP 5:

Add curry leaves, ginger-garlic paste, and green chillies. Saute for a few seconds.

STEP 6:

Add red chilli powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.

STEP 7:

Add the cooked chana dal along with its water. Mix well.

STEP 8:

Cook for 5-6 minutes on medium flame or until the dal thickens.

STEP 9:

Serve hot with rice or roti.
