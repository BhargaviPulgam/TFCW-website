Mirchi Bajji

Mirchi bajji is a popular Indian snack, particularly in the southern states of India. The resulting snack is spicy, crispy on the outside, and soft on the inside. It is often served with a chutney made of tamarind or mint, and is commonly sold in street food stalls or restaurants. Mirchi bajji is a popular snack during festivals, especially during the monsoon season when the hot and spicy snack pairs well with a cup of hot tea.

And this is how my mum makes Mirchi Bajji...

BASIC INGREDIENTS:

Large green chili peppers (such as Anaheim or banana peppers)

Gram flour (besan)

Rice flour

Salt

Red chili powder

Jwain (carom seeds)

Cumin powder

Coriander powder

Baking soda

Water

Oil for frying

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

6-8 long green chillies (bhavnagri or banana peppers)

1 cup gram flour (besan)

1/4 cup rice flour

1/2 tsp red chilli powder

1/2 tsp cumin powder

1/2 tsp coriander powder

1/4 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)

A pinch of baking soda

Salt to taste

Water as needed

Oil for deep frying

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Wash the green chillies and pat them dry. Cut off the stem and make a long slit along the length of each chilli, taking care not to cut through.

STEP 2:

In a mixing bowl, add the gram flour, rice flour, red chilli powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, carom seeds, baking soda, and salt. Mix well.

STEP 3:

Gradually add water and mix to form a smooth, thick batter. The batter should not be too runny or too thick.

STEP 4:

Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.

STEP 5:

Dip each chilli in the batter and coat it well. Shake off any excess batter and carefully drop the chilli into the hot oil.

STEP 6:

Fry the chillies on both sides until golden brown and crisp. Use a slotted spoon to remove the bajjis from the oil and place them on a paper towel to remove excess oil.

STEP 7:

Serve hot with coconut chutney or tomato ketchup.
