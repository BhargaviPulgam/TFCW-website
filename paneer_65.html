Paneer-65

Paneer-65 is a popular Indian appetizer that originated in Chennai. It is a vegetarian version of Chicken 65, which is a popular non-vegetarian dish. Paneer, also known as Indian cottage cheese, is the star ingredient of this dish. It is a rich source of protein and calcium, making it an ideal ingredient for vegetarians and people who avoid meat. The spices used in the marinade and the batter, such as ginger, garlic, red chili powder, and garam masala, not only add flavor but also have various health benefits Paneer-65 is a popular dish in Indian restaurants and is often served as an appetizer or a side dish with biryani or other main courses. It is a favorite among vegetarians and is loved for its spicy, tangy, and crispy taste.

And this is how my mum makes Paneer-65...

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

250g paneer, cut into bite-sized cubes

1 green bell pepper (capsicum), diced

1 onion, thinly sliced

2 tbsp corn flour

2 tbsp rice flour

1 tbsp all-purpose flour (maida)

1 tsp ginger garlic paste

1 tsp red chilli powder

1/2 tsp turmeric powder

1 tsp cumin powder

1 tsp coriander powder

1/2 tsp garam masala powder

Salt to taste

Oil for deep frying

Curry leaves and coriander leaves for garnish

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

In a mixing bowl, combine corn flour, rice flour, all-purpose flour, ginger garlic paste, red chilli powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala powder, and salt. Mix well and add water little by little to form a smooth batter.

STEP 2:

Add the paneer cubes and diced capsicum to the batter and mix well until they are well coated with the batter.

STEP 3:

Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the coated paneer and capsicum cubes in batches and deep fry until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove from the oil and drain excess oil on a paper towel.

STEP 4:

In a separate pan, heat 1 tbsp oil and add sliced onions. Saut√© until the onions turn translucent.

STEP 5:

Add the fried paneer and capsicum cubes to the pan and toss well to coat with the onions. Cook for 2-3 minutes.

STEP 6:

Garnish with curry leaves and coriander leaves.

STEP 7:

Serve hot as a snack or appetizer.
