SEMIYA PAYASAM

Semiya payasam is a traditional Indian sweet dish made with vermicelli, milk, sugar, and flavored with cardamom and sometimes garnished with nuts like cashews and raisins. It is typically served as a dessert or as a part of a meal during festivals or special occasions like weddings. Semiya payasam is a popular dish in South India and is also known as vermicelli kheer or sevai kheer in different regions. It is a creamy and delicious dessert that is loved by people of all ages.

The following is how my aunt makes Semiya payasam...

BASIC INGREDIENTS:

Vermicelli (semiya)

Ghee

Milk

Water

Sugar

Cardamom powder

Cashews (optional)

Raisins (optional)

Saffron (optional)

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

Vermicelli (Semiya) - 1 cup

Milk - 4 cups

Sugar - 3/4 to 1 cup (adjust to taste)

Ghee - 2 tablespoons

Cashews - 2 tablespoons

Raisins - 1 tablespoon

Cardamom powder - 1/2 teaspoon

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan and add cashews and raisins. Fry them until they turn golden brown and keep aside.

STEP 2:

In the same pan, roast the vermicelli until it turns light brown and keep aside.

STEP 3:

In a separate pot, heat milk and bring it to a boil.

STEP 4:

Add the roasted vermicelli to the boiling milk and stir well.

STEP 5:

Cook the vermicelli in milk for around 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vermicelli is fully cooked and the milk has thickened.

STEP 6:

Add sugar to the pan and stir well until it dissolves.

STEP 7:

Add the fried cashews and raisins to the pan and stir well.

STEP 8:

Finally, add cardamom powder and mix well.

STEP 9:

Turn off the heat and let the semiya payasam cool for a few minutes before serving.
