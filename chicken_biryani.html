CHICKEN BIRYANI

Hyderabadi chicken biryani is a popular dish from the city of Hyderabad in India. It is a rich and flavorful rice dish that is made with marinated chicken, aromatic spices, and long-grain basmati rice. Hyderabadi chicken biryani is a favorite among biryani lovers and is often served at special occasions like weddings and festivals. There are many different regional variations of chicken biryani in India, each with their own unique spices and ingredients.

The following is how my family makes Chicken Biryani...

BASIC INGREDIENTS:

Chicken

Basmati rice

Onions

Tomatoes

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

500g chicken, cut into pieces

2 cups basmati rice

3 medium-sized onions, thinly sliced

2 medium-sized tomatoes, chopped

1/2 cup yogurt

2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste

4 green chilies, slit length wise

1/2 cup mint leaves, chopped

1/2 cup coriander leaves, chopped

2 tsp garam masala powder

2 tsp red chili powder

1/2 tsp turmeric powder

1 tsp cumin seeds

3-4 cardamom pods

1-inch cinnamon stick

2 bay leaves

4-5 cloves

A pinch of saffron

1/4 cup oil

1/4 cup ghee

Salt, to taste

4 cups water

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Rinse the basmati rice with cold water and soak it for at least 30 minutes.

STEP 2:

In a large pot, heat the oil and ghee over medium-high heat.

STEP 3:

Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.

STEP 4:

Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute.

STEP 5:

Add the chopped tomatoes and green chilies, and cook until the tomatoes are soft.

STEP 6:

Add the chicken pieces and sauté until they are lightly browned.

STEP 7:

Add the yogurt, mint leaves, coriander leaves, garam masala powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt, and mix well.

STEP 8:

Add 1/2 cup of water and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the chicken cook for 15-20 minutes, or until it is tender.

STEP 9:

In a separate pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the soaked basmati rice, along with the cumin seeds, cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, cloves, and a pinch of salt.

STEP 10:

Stir well, reduce the heat to low, and cover the pot. Cook the rice until it is about 75% done, then drain the excess water.

STEP 11:

Layer the cooked chicken mixture over the rice in the pot, then sprinkle saffron on top.

STEP 12:

Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for another 10-15 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and fluffy.

STEP 13:

Once the biryani is cooked, fluff the rice with a fork and serve hot with raita or any other side dish of your choice.
