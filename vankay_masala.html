VANKAYA MASALA

Vankaya masala, also known as brinjal masala, is a popular South Indian dish made with eggplant, onions, tomatoes, and a blend of spices. The dish is usually served with rice or roti and is a favorite among vegetarians and meat-eaters alike. The combination of the slightly smoky eggplant with the tangy tomatoes and aromatic spices creates a delicious and satisfying meal.

The following is how my family makes Vankaya masala...

BASIC INGREDIENTS:

Eggplant (Brinjal)

Onions

Tomatoes

Ginger-garlic paste

Green chilies

Red chili powder

Turmeric powder

Coriander powder

Cumin powder

Garam masala

Salt

Oil

Mustard seeds

Cumin seeds

Curry leaves

Cashews

Coconut or Poppy seeds

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

Eggplant (Vankaya) - 2 medium-sized

Onion - 1 large, finely chopped

Tomatoes - 2 medium-sized, finely chopped

Green chilies - 2-3, slit lengthwise

Ginger-garlic paste - 1 tablespoon

Turmeric powder - 1/2 teaspoon

Red chili powder - 1 teaspoon

Coriander powder - 1 teaspoon

Cumin powder - 1/2 teaspoon

Garam masala - 1/2 teaspoon

Salt - to taste

Oil - 2-3 tablespoons

Mustard seeds - 1/2 teaspoon

Cumin seeds - 1/2 teaspoon

Curry leaves - 1 sprig

Cashews - 1/4 cup, soaked in water for 15 minutes (optional)

Coconut or Poppy seeds - 2 tablespoons, soaked in water for 15 minutes (optional)

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Rinse and pat dry the eggplants. Cut them into small cubes and set aside.

STEP 2:

Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds. Once they start to splutter, add cumin seeds and curry leaves.

STEP 3:

Add chopped onions and slit green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.

STEP 4:

Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.

STEP 5:

Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.

STEP 6:

Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.

STEP 7:

Add the chopped eggplants and mix well. Cover and cook until the eggplants are soft and tender.

STEP 8:

Meanwhile, drain the soaked cashews and coconut/poppy seeds. Grind them to a smooth paste using a little water.

STEP 9:

Once the eggplants are cooked, add the cashew-coconut/poppy seed paste and mix well.

STEP 10:

Let the masala simmer for a few minutes until it thickens and the flavors are well incorporated.

STEP 11:

Serve hot with steamed rice or chapati.
