CHOLE MASALA

Chole masala is a popular vegetarian dish from the Indian subcontinent, particularly in the northern region of India. The dish is usually served with rice, naan bread, or other flatbreads. Chole masala is a great source of protein and dietary fiber, and is also rich in essential vitamins and minerals. It is a filling and satisfying meal that is enjoyed by many, both in India and around the world. It can be made in a variety of ways, with some recipes including additional ingredients such as ginger, garlic, or yogurt to add extra depth and creaminess to the dish.

The following is how my family makes Chole masala...

BASIC INGREDIENTS:

Chickpeas (garbanzo beans)

Onion

Tomatoes

Garlic

Ginger

Green chilies

Cumin seeds

Coriander powder

Turmeric powder

Garam masala

Amchur (dry mango powder)

Red chili powder

Salt

Oil

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

2 cups chickpeas, soaked overnight and boiled until tender

2 onions, finely chopped

2 tomatoes, finely chopped

1 green chili, finely chopped

1 tsp ginger paste

1 tsp garlic paste

1 tsp cumin seeds

1 tsp coriander powder

1 tsp cumin powder

1 tsp red chili powder

1/2 tsp turmeric powder

1 tsp garam masala powder

Salt to taste

2 tbsp oil

2 tbsp fresh coriander leaves, finely chopped

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds.

STEP 2:

Once the cumin seeds start spluttering, add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.

STEP 3:

Add ginger paste and garlic paste, and sauté for a minute.

STEP 4:

Add chopped tomatoes and green chili, and cook until the tomatoes turn soft and mushy.

STEP 5:

Add coriander powder, cumin powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.

STEP 6:

Add boiled chickpeas and mix well with the masala. Add 1 cup of water, cover the pan with a lid, and let it simmer on medium heat for 10-15 minutes or until the gravy thickens.

STEP 7:

Add garam masala powder and mix well.

STEP 8:

Finally, garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or chapati.
