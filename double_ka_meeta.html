DOUBLE KA MEETA

Double ka meetha is a popular Indian dessert that originated in Hyderabad, Telangana. It is made using slices of bread that are deep-fried in ghee until crispy and golden brown. The dessert is believed to have been introduced to Hyderabad by the Nizams, the rulers of Hyderabad in the 18th century. Double ka meetha is a popular dessert during special occasions and festivals, especially during the Muslim holy month of Ramadan. It is also commonly served at weddings and other celebrations. The dish is known for its rich and sweet flavor and is often enjoyed with a cup of chai or coffee.

The following is how my aunt makes Double ka meeta...

BASIC INGREDIENTS:

Bread Slices

Milk

Sugar

Ghee

Cardamom Powder

Almonds (sliced)

Cashews (chopped)

Raisins

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

Bread slices - 10

Ghee - 1/2 cup

Milk - 2 cups

Sugar - 1 cup

Saffron - a pinch

Cardamom powder - 1/2 tsp

Almonds, Pistachios, and Cashews - sliced, for garnish

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Trim the edges of the bread slices and cut them into small squares.

STEP 2:

Heat ghee in a pan and fry the bread pieces until golden brown. Drain the excess ghee and keep aside.

STEP 3:

In another pan, boil milk and sugar together until the sugar dissolves completely. Add a pinch of saffron and cardamom powder and mix well.

STEP 4:

Now add the fried bread pieces to the milk mixture and let it cook on low flame for 5-7 minutes or until the bread pieces have soaked up the milk and become soft.

STEP 5:

Turn off the heat and let the mixture cool for 10-15 minutes.

STEP 6:

Garnish with sliced almonds, pistachios, and cashews before serving.

STEP 7:

Serve warm or chilled as desired.
