Undralu

Undrallu is a traditional South Indian dish made with rice flour. It is a small, round, and white dumpling-like food that is commonly served as a snack or during festivals. Undralu is a popular dish in many parts of India and is often prepared as a prasadam (offering) during Hindu festivals such as Ganesh Chaturthi, which is celebrated in honor of Lord Ganesha. Undralu is believed to be one of Lord Ganesha's favorite foods, and it is commonly offered to him during puja (worship) rituals.

And this is how my mum makes Undralu...

BASIC INGREDIENTS:

Biyyam pindi (rice flour)

Nuvvulu (sesame seeds)

Nune (salt)

Water

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

1 cup rice

Water, as needed

Salt, to taste

Oil, for deep frying

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Rinse the rice well and soak it in water for 3-4 hours.

STEP 2:

Drain the water completely and spread the rice on a clean cloth or paper towel to dry for about 30 minutes.

STEP 3:

Grind the rice into a fine powder in a mixer grinder or food processor.

STEP 4:

In a pan, dry roast the rice powder on low flame for 5-6 minutes until it turns slightly brown in color.

STEP 5:

Transfer the roasted rice powder to a mixing bowl and add salt to taste. Mix well.

STEP 6:

Add water little by little and knead the mixture into a dough. The dough should be firm and not sticky.

STEP 7:

Take small portions of the dough and roll them into small balls (about the size of a marble).

STEP 8:

Heat oil in a deep frying pan on medium heat.

STEP 9:

Once the oil is hot, add the rice balls to the oil and fry them until they turn golden brown in color.

STEP 10:

Remove the fried balls from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on a paper towel.

STEP 11:

Allow the undralu to cool down completely and store in an airtight container.
