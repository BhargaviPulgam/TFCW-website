Pesarapappu

Pesarpappu, also known as moong dal, is a type of lentil commonly used in Indian cuisine. It is small and yellow in color, and has a mild, nutty flavor. Moong dal is a rich source of protein, fiber, and various vitamins and minerals. It contains high levels of folate, iron, potassium, and magnesium, which are essential for maintaining good health. It is also low in fat and cholesterol, making it a great food option for those trying to maintain a healthy weight. It is said to be good for digestion and can help regulate blood sugar levels. It is also believed to be beneficial for the skin and hair, and is often used in various beauty treatments.

And this is how my family makes Pesarapappu...

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

1 cup moong dal (pesarapappu)

2 medium-sized tomatoes, chopped

1 onion, chopped

2-3 green chillies, slipt

1-inch ginger, chopped

4-5 garlic cloves, chopped

A few curry leaves

1 tsp cumin seeds

1 tsp mustard seeds

1/4 tsp turmeric powder

1 tsp red chilli powder

1 tsp coriander powder

Salt to taste

Water as required

2-3 tbsp oil

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Rinse the moong dal well and soak it in water for 30 minutes.

STEP 2:

In a pan or kadhai, heat oil over medium flame.

STEP 3:

Add the cumin seeds and mustard seeds, and let them splutter.

STEP 4:

Add the chopped onions, ginger, garlic, green chillies, and curry leaves. Saute until the onions turn translucent.

STEP 5:

Add the chopped tomatoes and saute until they turn soft and mushy.

STEP 6:

Add the turmeric powder, red chilli powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and saute for a minute.

STEP 7:

Drain the soaked moong dal and add it to the pan. Mix well.

STEP 8:

Add enough water to cover the dal and bring it to a boil.

STEP 9:

Once the dal starts boiling, reduce the flame to low and cover the pan with a lid.

STEP 10:

Let the dal cook for about 20-25 minutes or until it becomes soft and mushy. Keep stirring in between to avoid sticking to the bottom.

STEP 11:

Once the dal is cooked, turn off the flame and let it cool for a few minutes.

STEP 12:

Using a hand blender or mixer grinder, blend the dal until it becomes smooth and creamy.

STEP 13:

Serve hot with steamed rice or roti.
