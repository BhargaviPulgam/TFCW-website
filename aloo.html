Aloo

Telugu aloo fry is a popular dish in the Telugu cuisine, which originated in the state of Telangana in South India. The dish is usually served as a side dish with steamed rice, dal, and sambar, but it can also be enjoyed as a snack on its own. Telugu aloo fry is a delicious and satisfying dish that can be enjoyed by people of all ages. It is a great way to add some variety to your meals and is perfect for those who are looking for a quick and easy dish that is full of flavor.

And this is how my family makes Aloo...

BASIC INGREDIENTS:

Potatoes, peeled and cubed

Oil

Mustard seeds

Cumin seeds

Urad dal

Chana dal

Asafoetida(hing)

Dried red chillies

Turmeric powder

Red chili powder

Salt

Ginger-garlic paste

Onions

Fresh coriander leaves, chopped(for garnish)

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

3-4 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and cubed

2 tbsp oil

1 tsp mustard seeds

1 tsp cumin seeds

1 tsp urad dal

1 tsp chana dal

A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

2-3 dried red chillies

1 tsp turmeric powder

1 tsp red chilli powder (optional)

Salt, to taste

1 tbsp ginger garlic paste

1 onion, chopped

Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.

STEP 2:

Add cumin seeds, urad dal, and chana dal. Fry until the dals turn golden brown.

STEP 3:

Add asafoetida and dried red chillies. Fry for a few seconds.

STEP 4:

Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.

STEP 5:

Add ginger garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.

STEP 6:

Add the cubed potatoes and mix well. Add turmeric powder, red chilli powder (if using), and salt to taste. Mix everything well.

STEP 7:

Cover the pan with a lid and cook the potatoes on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally until they are cooked through and tender.

STEP 8:

Once the potatoes are cooked, increase the heat and fry them for a few more minutes, stirring occasionally, until they turn golden brown and crispy.

STEP 9:

Turn off the heat and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

STEP 10:

Serve hot as a side dish with rice, roti or any Indian bread of your choice.
