Ugadhi Pacchadi

Ugadi Pachadi is a special dish made during the Ugadi festival, which is celebrated as the Telugu New Year in the southern states of India. It is a unique and symbolic dish that represents the different flavors of life - sweet, sour, bitter, salty, and spicy. Ugadi Pachadi is made with six different ingredients, each representing a different taste and meaning. Jaggery is added for sweetness, tamarind for sourness, neem flowers for bitterness, salt for saltiness, green chili for spiciness, and raw mango for tanginess. All the ingredients are mixed together in a bowl to create a delicious and complex flavor that represents the different experiences of life. It is believed that eating Ugadi Pachadi on the day of Ugadi brings good luck, prosperity, and happiness in life. It also reminds people of the ups and downs of life, and to accept them with equanimity.

And this is how my mum makes Ugadhi Pacchadi...

BASIC INGREDIENTS:

Neem flowers

Raw mango

Tamarind

Jaggery

Banana

Salt

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

Raw Mangoes - 1 cup (finely chopped)

Jaggery - 1 cup (grated)

Tamarind - 1/4 cup (soaked in water and extract juice)

Neem flowers - 2 tablespoons

Coconut pieces - 2 tablespoons (optional)

Banana - 1 (sliced)

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

In a mixing bowl, add the finely chopped raw mangoes.

STEP 2:

Add the grated jaggery to the mangoes and mix well until the jaggery dissolves.

STEP 3:

Extract the tamarind juice by soaking the tamarind in water and squeezing out the juice. Add the tamarind juice to the mixing bowl.

STEP 4:

Add the neem flowers and coconut pieces to the mixing bowl and mix well.

STEP 5:

Lastly, add the sliced banana to the mixing bowl and mix gently.

STEP 6:

Let the mixture sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to blend together.

STEP 7:

Serve Ugadi Pacchadi as a traditional dish during the Ugadi festival.
