Spring onion pakodi

Spring onion pakodi is a popular Indian snack made from spring onions, gram flour, and a blend of spices. Also known as Hara Pyaz Pakora, it is a deep-fried fritter that has a crispy exterior and a soft, flavorful interior. The dish is commonly served as a tea-time snack or as a starter/appetizer in Indian cuisine. Spring onion pakodi is not only delicious but also nutritious, as spring onions are a good source of vitamins A and C, while gram flour is a good source of protein and dietary fiber. However, due to the deep-frying process, it is high in calories and should be consumed in moderation as part of a balanced diet.

And this is how my mum makes Spring Onion Pakodi...

BASIC INGREDIENTS:

Spring onions, finely chopped

Besan (gram flour)

Rice flour

Ginger, grated

Garlic, minced

Green chilies, finely chopped

Red chili powder

Turmeric powder

Coriander powder

Cumin powder

Salt

Baking soda

Chaat masala

Lemon juice

Oil, for deep frying

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

1 cup besan (gram flour)

1/4 cup rice flour

1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder

1 teaspoon red chili powder

1 teaspoon cumin seeds

1 teaspoon coriander seeds

1 teaspoon ginger, grated

1 teaspoon garlic, minced

2-3 green chilies, finely chopped

1 teaspoon chaat masala

1 tablespoon lemon juice

Salt, to taste

Water, as needed

Oil, for deep frying

1 bunch spring onions, finely chopped

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

In a mixing bowl, combine besan, rice flour, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin seeds, coriander seeds, ginger, garlic, green chilies, chaat masala, lemon juice, and salt. Mix well.

STEP 2:

Add water gradually to the mixture and whisk until you get a smooth and thick batter. The batter should be of pouring consistency.

STEP 3:

Heat oil for deep frying in a kadai or a deep frying pan.

STEP 4:

Add finely chopped spring onions to the batter and mix well.

STEP 5:

Once the oil is hot, reduce the flame to medium. Using a spoon or your fingers, drop small portions of the batter into the hot oil.

STEP 6:

Fry the pakodas on medium flame until they turn golden brown in color and crispy.

STEP 7:

Using a slotted spoon, remove the pakodas from the oil and drain them on paper towels.

STEP 8:

Serve hot with mint chutney or tomato ketchup.
