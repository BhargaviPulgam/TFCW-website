PARAMANNAM

Gulab jamun is a popular dessert in Indian cuisine, made by deep-frying small balls made of a dough of milk solids (khoya) and flour, which are then soaked in a sweet syrup flavored with cardamom, rose water, or saffron. The name "gulab jamun" comes from two words: "gulab," which means rose water (used in the syrup), and "jamun," which refers to a small, dark fruit with a similar shape to the dessert. Gulab jamun is often served at festivals, weddings, and other special occasions, and is considered a traditional and classic dessert in many parts of India, as well as in neighboring countries like Pakistan and Bangladesh. It can be served warm or at room temperature, and is often garnished with chopped nuts like almonds or pistachios.

The following is how my aunt makes Paramannam...

BASIC INGREDIENTS:

Rice

Milk

Jaggery or sugar

Ghee

Cashews

Raisins

Cardamom powder

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

Rice - 1 cup

Milk - 4 cups

Jaggery - 1 cup, grated

Ghee - 1/4 cup

Cashews - 10-12

Raisins - 10-12

Cardamom powder - 1/2 tsp

Water - 2 cups

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Wash the rice and soak it in water for 30 minutes.

STEP 2:

Drain the water and grind the rice coarsely using a blender or mixer grinder.

STEP 3:

In a large pot, heat milk and water together until it comes to a boil.

STEP 4:

Add the ground rice to the boiling milk and stir continuously to prevent lumps.

STEP 5:

Cook the mixture on medium flame until the rice is fully cooked and the milk thickens, stirring occasionally.

STEP 6:

Add the grated jaggery to the pot and stir until it melts and blends well with the rice and milk mixture.

STEP 7:

In a separate pan, heat ghee and add cashews and raisins. Fry until golden brown.

STEP 8:

Add the fried cashews, raisins, and cardamom powder to the paramannam mixture and mix well.

STEP 9:

Turn off the flame and let the paramannam cool down for 10-15 minutes.

STEP 10:

Serve the delicious paramannam hot or cold.
