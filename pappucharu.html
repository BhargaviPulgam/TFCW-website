Pappucharu

Pappu charu is a popular South Indian lentil-based stew that is typically made with toor dal (pigeon peas), tamarind, and a variety of vegetables such as munakaya (drumstick), dosakaya (yellow cucumber), and carrots. The dal is cooked until it is soft and mushy and then combined with the vegetables and a blend of spices to create a flavorful and hearty stew. Toor dal is a good source of protein, fiber, and various vitamins and minerals, such as folate, iron, and potassium. Munakaya (drumstick) is a rich source of vitamin C, iron, and calcium. Dosakaya (yellow cucumber) is a low-calorie vegetable that is high in vitamin C and fiber. Carrots are an excellent source of beta-carotene, vitamin K1, potassium, and fiber.

And this is how my family makes Pappucharu...

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

1 cup toor dal (pigeon pea lentils)

4-5 cups tamarind water

2-3 munakkaya (drumsticks), chopped into 2 inch pieces

1 dosakaya (yellow cucumber), chopped into small pieces

1 carrot, chopped into small pieces

1 onion, chopped

2 tomatoes, chopped

3-4 green chillies, slit lengthwise

1 tsp red chilli powder

1/2 tsp turmeric powder

2 tsp coriander powder

1 tsp cumin powder

5-6 garlic cloves, minced

1 inch ginger, minced

A handful of curry leaves

1 tsp mustard seeds

1 tsp cumin seeds

1/4 tsp fenugreek seeds

A pinch of asafoetida

Salt to taste

3-4 tbsp oil

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Rinse the toor dal in water and soak for 30 minutes. Drain the water and add the dal to a pot with 4 cups of water.

STEP 2:

Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 30-40 minutes until the dal is soft and cooked.

STEP 3:

In a separate pot, add the tamarind water, chopped munakkaya, dosakaya, and carrot.

STEP 4:

Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes until the vegetables are cooked.

STEP 5:

In a pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, and asafoetida. Let the seeds splutter.

STEP 6:

Add chopped onions, green chillies, and curry leaves. Saute until the onions turn golden brown.

STEP 7:

Add minced garlic and ginger. Saute for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell goes away.

STEP 8:

Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.

STEP 9:

Add the cooked toor dal to the pot with vegetables and tamarind water. Mix well.

STEP 10:

Add red chilli powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt to taste. Mix well and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes until the flavors are well combined.

STEP 11:

Add the tempering (mustard seeds, cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, asafoetida, onions, green chillies, curry leaves, garlic, and ginger) to the pot with pappu charu. Mix well.

STEP 12:

Serve hot with rice and papadams. Enjoy!
