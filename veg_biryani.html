VEG BIRYANI

Paneer biryani is a vegetarian version of the traditional biryani that is made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese) and rice. It is a popular dish among vegetarians in India and is commonly found on the menu at Indian restaurants. Paneer biryani is usually served with raita (a yogurt-based side dish) and salad, and can also be enjoyed with papadums (crispy Indian flatbread) and chutney. Some variations of the dish may include additional ingredients such as saffron, mint, and coriander leaves to enhance the flavors and aromas. Overall, paneer biryani is a flavorful and satisfying vegetarian alternative to traditional meat-based biryanis.

For one person the measurements and procedure for Telugu Chicken Biryani

Paneer (cottage cheese), cubed

2 cups basmati rice, soaked in water for 30 minutes

2 onions, thinly sliced

2 tomatoes, chopped

1/2 cup yogurt

2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste

4 green chilies, slit length wise

1/2 cup mint leaves, chopped

1/2 cup coriander leaves, chopped

2 tsp garam masala powder

2 tsp red chili powder

1/2 tsp turmeric powder

1 tsp cumin seeds

3-4 cardamom pods

1-inch cinnamon stick

2 bay leaves

4-5 cloves

A pinch of saffron

1/4 cup oil

1/4 cup ghee

Salt, to taste

4 cups water

PROCEDURE

STEP 1:

Wash the basmati rice thoroughly in water and soak it in water for at least 30 minutes.

STEP 2:

In a large pot, heat oil and ghee over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, bay leaves, cinnamon sticks, cardamom pods, and cloves. Sauté for a minute or until fragrant.

STEP 3:

Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.

STEP 4:

Add chopped carrots, green beans, green peas, cauliflower florets, diced potatoes, bell peppers, and sliced mushrooms. Sauté for 2-3 minutes.

STEP 5:

Add slit green chillies and ginger garlic paste. Sauté for another 2-3 minutes.

STEP 6:

Add chopped mint leaves and coriander leaves. Mix well.

STEP 7:

Add garam masala powder and salt to taste. Mix well.

STEP 8:

Add yogurt and mix well.

STEP 9:

Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pot. Mix gently.

STEP 10:

Add enough water to cover the rice by about 1 inch. Mix well.

STEP 11:

Add a pinch of saffron to milk and let it soak for a few minutes.

STEP 12:

Pour the saffron milk over the rice.

STEP 13:

Cover the pot with a lid and cook on low heat for about 15-20 minutes or until the rice is fully cooked.

STEP 14:

Turn off the heat and let the biryani rest for 5-10 minutes.

STEP 15:

Fluff up the biryani with a fork and serve hot with raita or any other side dish of your choice.
